# Nutrition for Women's Health

## Chapter Summary
Nutrition is the foundation of hormonal balance and long-term vitality. This chapter focuses on the specific nutrient needs of women, cycle-syncing your diet, and foods that fight inflammation.

## Medical Explanation: Unique Nutritional Needs
Women have distinct nutritional requirements due to menstruation, pregnancy, and menopause.

### Key Nutrients
1.  **Iron:**
    *   **Why:** Essential to replace blood lost during menstruation.
    *   **Sources:** Red meat, spinach, lentils, pumpkin seeds. Pair with Vitamin C for better absorption.
2.  **Calcium:**
    *   **Why:** Critical for bone density, especially to prevent osteoporosis later in life.
    *   **Sources:** Dairy, fortified plant milks, leafy greens, almonds.
3.  **Folate (Vitamin B9):**
    *   **Why:** DNA repair and crucial for reproductive health.
    *   **Sources:** Dark leafy greens, beans, citrus fruits.
4.  **Magnesium:**
    *   **Why:** The "relaxation mineral." Helps with cramps, sleep, and mood.
    *   **Sources:** Dark chocolate, avocados, nuts, seeds.

## Cycle-Syncing Nutrition
Adjusting your diet to match your hormonal phases.

*   **Menstrual Phase:** Focus on iron-rich and warming foods (soups, stews) to replenish energy.
*   **Follicular Phase:** Fresh, light foods like salads, fermented foods (kimchi, yogurt) to support rising energy.
*   **Ovulation Phase:** Fiber-rich foods (fruits, veggies) to help the body eliminate excess estrogen.
*   **Luteal Phase:** Complex carbs (sweet potatoes, brown rice) to curb cravings and stabilize serotonin. Magnesium-rich foods for PMS.

## Anti-Inflammatory Diet
Chronic inflammation drives many issues like PCOS and endometriosis.
*   **Eat More:** Berries, fatty fish (salmon), turmeric, ginger, olive oil, green tea.
*   **Limit:** Processed sugars, trans fats, excessive alcohol.

## Diagnosis & Clinical Care
*   **Deficiency Testing:** Check Vitamin D, B12, and Ferritin (iron storage) levels annually.
*   **Gut Health:** A healthy microbiome is essential for hormone regulation.

## Precautions & Home Care
*   **Hydration:** Water is vital for every cellular process. Aim for 2-3 liters daily.
*   **Mindful Eating:** Focus on nourishment rather than restriction.
*   **Meal Prep:** Preparing healthy staples in advance reduces reliance on processed convenience foods.
